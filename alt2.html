Yes, everyone can benefit from basic estate planning. Without a plan, state laws will determine how your assets are distributed and who makes decisions for you if you become incapacitated. An estate plan ensures your wishes are honored and makes things much easier for your loved ones.

Even if you don't have significant wealth, you likely have important decisions to make about healthcare, guardianship of minor children, and distribution of personal belongings. We can help you create a plan that fits your situation and budget.

Our fees vary based on the complexity of your needs, but we always provide clear, upfront pricing before you commit to anything. During your free consultation, we'll discuss your situation and give you an accurate estimate.

Basic estate planning packages typically start at a few hundred dollars, while more comprehensive plans involving trusts may cost more. The investment in proper planning often saves families thousands of dollars in probate costs and legal fees later.

A Will is a legal document that specifies how your assets should be distributed after you pass away. It goes through probate court, which is a public process and can take several months.

A Trust is a legal arrangement where you transfer assets to be managed by a trustee for the benefit of your beneficiaries. Trusts avoid probate, remain private, and can provide more control over how and when assets are distributed.

Many people benefit from having both. We'll help you understand which option (or combination) is best for your circumstances during our consultation.

For most clients, the entire process takes 2-4 weeks from your initial consultation to signing your completed documents. This timeline includes:

  • • Initial consultation and information gathering (1 week)
  • • Document drafting and review (1-2 weeks)
  • • Final meeting and signing (same day)

We work at a pace that's comfortable for you. If you need documents prepared more quickly due to travel plans or health concerns, we can often accommodate rush requests.

Absolutely! In fact, we recommend reviewing your estate plan every 3-5 years or whenever you experience major life changes such as:

  • • Marriage, divorce, or remarriage
  • • Birth or adoption of children or grandchildren
  • • Significant changes in assets or financial situation
  • • Moving to a different state
  • • Changes in tax laws

Updates are typically simple and cost-effective. We're always here to help you keep your plan current and effective.

Without an estate plan, your state's intestacy laws will determine what happens to your assets and who makes decisions for you. This can lead to several challenges:

  • • Your assets may not go to the people you would have chosen
  • • The probate process becomes longer and more expensive
  • • Family disputes may arise over your belongings
  • • Court-appointed guardians may make healthcare decisions
  • • Your loved ones face additional stress during an already difficult time

Having a plan in place, even a simple one, provides clarity, reduces costs, and gives your family peace of mind.
