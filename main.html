FAQS

Have Questions?

Our FAQ section covers everything you need to know about LPA's, from setup to troubleshooting and support. Find quick, helpful answers to help you make your decision.

A lasting power of attorney is a legal document that allows you to appoint one or more people to make decisions on your behalf during your lifetime.
An LPA is set up by you while you have mental capacity. A Deputyship is appointed by the Court of Protection for someone who has already lost capacity.
There are two types: one for health and welfare, and another for property and financial affairs. You can choose to make one or both.
The process can take several months, as it needs to be registered with the Office of the Public Guardian (OPG) before it can be used.
