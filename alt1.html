Frequently Asked Questions

Clear answers to common questions about our services

Our simple single will starts from £195, and mirror wills for couples from £295. These are fixed fees with no hidden costs. More complex wills involving trusts or business assets are priced individually, but we'll always provide a clear quote before starting work. All prices include a free initial consultation and secure document storage.

Without a will, your estate is distributed according to intestacy rules, which may not reflect your wishes. Your spouse may not inherit everything, unmarried partners receive nothing, and the process can be lengthy and stressful for your family. Making a will ensures your assets go exactly where you want them to and can significantly reduce stress for your loved ones.

Typically, probate takes 6-12 months to complete, though complex estates may take longer. Factors affecting timing include the estate's complexity, whether there's a valid will, potential disputes, and how quickly we can gather information. We handle all aspects efficiently to minimize delays while ensuring everything is done correctly. We'll keep you informed at every stage.

An LPA is highly recommended for everyone over 18. It allows trusted individuals to make decisions on your behalf if you lose mental capacity. Without one, your family may need to apply to the Court of Protection, which is expensive, time-consuming, and stressful. An LPA gives you control over who makes decisions and provides peace of mind for you and your family.

Yes, absolutely. We recommend reviewing your will every 3-5 years or after major life events (marriage, divorce, births, deaths, or significant asset changes). Minor changes can be made via a codicil (from £125), while major changes may require a new will. We offer free will storage and can quickly arrange updates whenever you need them.

Our full probate service includes: obtaining the grant of probate, collecting and valuing assets, paying debts and taxes, preparing estate accounts, and distributing assets to beneficiaries. We handle all correspondence with banks, HMRC, and other institutions. You'll have a dedicated solicitor managing your case and regular updates on progress.

Yes, we offer home visits throughout our service area at no extra charge. This is particularly helpful for elderly clients, those with mobility issues, or anyone who prefers the comfort of their own home. We can also arrange evening and weekend appointments to fit around your schedule. Just mention your preference when booking your consultation.

There are several legitimate ways to reduce inheritance tax, including using your nil-rate band, making lifetime gifts, setting up trusts, leaving assets to charity, and utilizing business or agricultural relief. The best strategy depends on your individual circumstances. We offer specialist estate planning consultations to help minimize your tax liability while ensuring your wishes are met.
